Reference 0010

Context, Escalation, and Provenance

Use this sheet to keep exact facts, recovery context, escalation decisions, and source attribution intact across long agent workflows.

The Decision

A long-running agent needs a controller-owned case file: structured state that survives summarization, subagent handoff, context compaction, and final synthesis.

Four Artifacts

  • Case facts: exact IDs, amounts, dates, statuses, user requests, policy results, and assumptions.
  • Error packets: failure type, attempted action, retryability, partial results, alternatives, and recovery hint.
  • Escalation rules: human request, policy gap, authority limit, contradiction, safety issue, or no progress.
  • Claim-source map: claim, source ID, excerpt or location, publication or collection date, and uncertainty status.

Context Rules

Persist

Exact values

Never summarize away amounts, dates, IDs, policy decisions, customer commitments, or source dates.

Trim

Verbose tools

Keep only fields needed for the current decision, audit trail, or future recovery.

Place

Important first

Put the current case facts near the beginning of the prompt, with detailed evidence in labeled sections.

Separate

Facts from chat

Use conversation history for flow; use structured state for facts that must remain stable.

Escalation Test

  • Escalate immediately when the user explicitly asks for a human.
  • Escalate when policy is silent, contradictory, or outside the agent's authority.
  • Ask for clarification when multiple records match; do not choose heuristically.
  • Do not use sentiment or self-reported confidence as the only escalation signal.
  • Include what was tried, what evidence exists, what is missing, and what decision is needed.

Review Question

Before summarizing or handing off, ask: what exact fact, error, source, date, or user instruction would be dangerous to blur?
